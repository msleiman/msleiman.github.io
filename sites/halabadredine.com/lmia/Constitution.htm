Constitution and by-laws

Lebanese Medical Informatics Association
Mission Statement
The Lebanese Medical Informatics Association is a non-profit, non-governmental organization based on the voluntary work of Health professionals and Computer Scientists with a special interest in Medical Informatics.
The purpose of the association is to promote the use of the Telecommunications and Information Technology in Medicine or, perhaps more accurately, in the Health sector. This technology is viewed as a means to improve the services offered by the Health Care Delivery System, decrease their cost and decrease loss of time in services’ delivery.
In Europe and elsewhere, during the last decades, information technologies have played a decisive role in the dramatic developments, not only in diagnosis, treatment, and surveillance, but also in the more collective aspects of healthcare and health prevention, such as clinical trials, Epidemiology, and health education. We are looking forward as The Lebanese Medical Informatics Association to a jumlaje  with The International Medical Association.
One of the most effective ways to promote the use of Information Technology is to educate Health Professionals. Amongst our activities, along these lines, are the organization of educational seminars, conferences, discussion groups and other activities that promote research, progress and enhancement of the application of information technology in the health sector. There is reason to believe that professionals who will not be able to absorb and use the new technology will not be able to stay current in their field and ultimately serve their patients as effectively as possible.
As we are proceeding towards the realization of LMIA as part of the IMIA, new legal and ethical issues are becoming more evident. We, as professionals, should participate in the formation of the ethical and legal framework for the use of information technology in the health sector.
Finally, in order to promote its purposes, LMIA will seek co-operation with local institutions, the relevant authorities in Lebanon especially The Ministry of Public Health and The Ministry of Higher Education as well as allied organizations and agencies of the European Union, USAID, IMIA and other countries.
Hala Badredine
Founding President LMIA
06 April 2006
Beirut - Liban

Constitution and by-laws 

1. NAME
A non-profit making, scientific association is hereby founded that shall hereinafter be known as the Lebanese Medical Informatics Association. Registered offices of the association shall be based in Hala Center, Nabatye - Lebanon.

 2. DEFINITIONS
In this document, the following terms have these definitions as indicated:
a) "Executive Committee"       refers to the association’s Executive committee.
b)"President"   refers to the elected president of the executive committee.
c) "Scientists" refers to persons holding a recognized University Degree.
d) "Committee" refers to any group or body designated by the Executive committee for the purpose of the study or promotion of any specific subject or matter concerning the Association.
 
 3. AIMS OF THE ASSOCIATION
a) The incorporation of communications technology in general in the health sector, including Telemedicine, multimedia, and the Internet, with a view to education of members of the medical and paramedical professions and health care practitioners and ultimately, improvements in the provision of patient care.

b) The provision of assistance towards the establishment of the appropriate legal and procedural infrastructure that will promote the applications of Informatics in the health sector
c) The organization of educational seminars, conferences, discussion groups and other activities that promote research, progress and the furtherance of the applications of communications technology in the health sector.

d) Co-operation with allied organizations and the local authorities Lebanon and other countries as well as IMIA.   
 

4. ASSOCIATION’S INCOME
Association funds and income shall be derived from subscriptions, enrolment fees, gifts, grants, subsidies, inheritances, income from association activities, or any other income from whatever source legally available to the association.
 
 5. MEMBERS
Members of the association are classified as:
a) Regular members
b) Corresponding members
c) Honorary members
a) Regular members may be:
Any member of any medical, paramedical or allied profession, or any health care practitioner pertaining to the field of Informatics who is a permanent resident of Lebanon and who expresses an interest in, and supports the aims of the association.
b) Corresponding Members may be:
Any medical, paramedical or allied profession, or any health care practitioner or scientist involved in the field of Informatics who is a permanent resident of a country other than Lebanon, who has an interest in informatics as applied to health sector, and who expresses an interest in, and supports the aims of the association.
c) Honorary Members may be:
Any person designated as such by the Executive committee in recognition of a significant contribution by them towards the association or its aims.
Every ordinary, Corresponding or honorary member has the right to submit, in writing to the Executive committee any matter or subject for study or for a resolution.
 
7. ADMISSION TO MEMBERSHIP
a) Enrollment of members :
Ordinary or Corresponding members are admitted to membership following the submission and approval by the executive committee, of the relevant written application form, the payment of an enrollment fee of 30,000 Lebanese Liras. Members may be required, in addition, to submit copies of their university degrees or other professional diplomas.
b) Subscription Fees

The annual subscription fee is designated as 30,000 Lebanese Liras. This amount may be increased or reduced following a decision of the association general assembly arrived at by simple ballot vote, following a recommendation by the executive committee.
Only ordinary members are required to pay an annual subscription fee.
c) Disqualification of members

A member can be expelled from the association following a decision by the association, if:
   
It is considered that her or his actions or behavior are at conflict with the aims or interests of the association.
It is implied that the member in question has a right to a hearing prior to any decision concerning his or her expulsion. Appeals against any such decision by the Association can be submitted by the member in question to the General Assembly of the association. 
 d) Resignation of members

Any member has the right to resign from the association at his or her discretion, providing that their subscription dues are in order.
 
8. CONTRIBUTORS & BENEFACTORS
The Association can designate, from time to time, contributors and benefactors of the association. The relevant procedures shall be dictated by the circumstances that apply in each case.
 
9. ELECTIONS AND THE RIGHT TO VOTE
Only ordinary members shall be entitled to vote and to be elected to membership of the Association. Elections to membership of the Association shall be by secret ballot.
 
10. RULING BODIES
Ruling bodies of the Association are the member's General Assembly and the Executive committee.
 
11. ORDINARY AND EXTRAORDINARY GENERAL ASSEMBLY MEETINGS
a) Ordinary General Assembly Meetings shall be convened during the 3rd week of the third month of every year by invitation sent to each member (by fax, mail or electronic mail) at least 15 days prior to the date set for the meeting, or by announcement in the Press. Both invitations and announcements in the Press must list the agenda and specify the date time and place of the meeting. The presence of at least one half the members of the Association at the meeting shall be considered a quorum. If no quorum is present, the meeting is postponed for 2 weeks and reconvened, in which case a quorum is considered to be present regardless of the number of members present.
b) Extraordinary General Assembly Meetings are convened by the Association whenever they see fit, or following on a written requisition by at least one fifth of the members of the Association provided they are up to date with their subscription obligations. Or ONLY, the president has the right to call upon a meeting of the general assembly in cases he/she finds necessary.
c) There is no election processes by proxy.
d) Except for those cases specified otherwise in the current constitution, resolutions during General Meetings are decided by majority vote of the members present, and in case of equality of votes the deciding vote rests with the President.
 e) Voting during General Meetings is by a show of hands, except when deemed otherwise in specific cases, or if the constitution specifies otherwise.
 f) Any resolution arrived at during a General Meeting is null and void if it is contrary to the Law or the Constitution of the Association.
 
12. EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE
a) The Executive committee shall consist of seven members.
b) Each nomination for election to membership of the Association must be proposed by 10 members of the Association. In order for the nomination to be valid, each candidate's subscription dues must be in order i.e. a member in good standing.
c) The 7 members of the Executive committee are elected by the assembly of the General Meeting as follows:
    i) During the Founding Meeting, seven members are elected to Association for the first 3 years period.
    ii) At the end of the three years period, a new Association consisting of seven members is elected at the Ordinary General Meeting for a three year period in office.
d) The members of the Association elect among themselves the President, Vice-President, Secretary and Treasurer.
e) In case of development of a vacancy in the Association, this vacancy shall be filled by the first runner-up of the last elections for the remainder of the Association's period in office. In case of development of vacancies in more than one half of the seats of the Association, an Extraordinary General Meeting is convened and elections are carried out in order to fill those seats.
f) The Association may from time to time, appoint members to Committees empowered to handle specific matters. Any member, whether ordinary, Corresponding or honorary, is eligible for appointment to such committees.
g) Any member of the Association may be expelled from his position for actions or behavior at conflict with the aims or interests of the association or the provisions of the Constitution, following a decision of the association that has a majority of two-thirds of its executive committee members or by the president him/herself. (5 votes)
It is implied that matters concerning the disqualification of a member of the Association are included in the agenda, and that the member in question is granted the right to a hearing prior to any decision concerning his or her expulsion.
h) The Executive committee is the highest governing body of the association, and exercises all such powers necessary for the management of the affairs of the association. 
In particular, the Association: 

i) Determines the general policies, the direction, and actions in general of the association so that it may  achieve its goals.
ii) Approves, prior to their submission to the General Assembly, the association's income and expenditure accounts and balance sheets as prepared by the Treasurer.
iii) Proposes, modifies, or rejects internal rules which regulate the function of the Association and the Committees. It is implied that internal regulations should not be contrary to the resolutions of the General Assembly or the Constitution of the Association.
iv) Has the power to appoint an employee or employees for the association, under terms and conditions that it deems appropriate for the good of the association.

 
DUTIES AND POWERS OF ASSOCIATION OFFICIALS
President of the Association
The President of the Association, together with the Secretary,  represents the Association in all its legal and judicial activities before any  Authority or Court and before any natural or legal person. The president convenes and presides over the meetings of the Association and General Assembly, is responsible for the publication of the annual report of the activities of the association during the tenure of his/her presidency, and is answerable during the General Meetings to the association members for the activities of the Association as well as the programs and activities of the association in general. He countersigns, together with the treasurer, all documents pertaining to association income or expenditure, as well as all bankers cheques for amounts greater than three million Lebanese Liras. During elections, he/she has the deciding vote in all cases of equality of votes. He/she has the right to call for an extraordinary general assembly meeting any time finds necessary.
Vice-President of the Association
The Vice-President replaces the President during his absence, and carries out any duties that are assigned to him by the President.  
Secretary
The Secretary a) represents, together with the president, the Association in all its dealings with any third persons b) countersigns, with the President, all association documents or writs c) keeps the minutes of all meetings of Association d) is responsible for the management of the Association's office activities and correspondence e) maintains a general register of members g) replaces the President during absences of the President and Vice-President.  
Treasurer

The treasurer  a) is responsible for the keeping of the associations books of account b) keeps receipts of  the associations income and expenditure c) keeps accurate records of the associations finances and acts according to the directions and resolutions of the association d) collects the subscription fees e) deposits in a bank in Lebanon chosen by the association and in the name of the Association every sum above three million Lebanese Liras, within 48 hours g) Draws up the Associations yearly financial statement and  submits it for approval to the Association and Annual General Meeting.

 

14. PROCEEDINGS OF THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

a)      Ordinary meetings of the association are convened by the President at least every month, while extraordinary meetings are convened by the President or following a request submitted to him/her by at least two members of the Association.
b)      Over half the members of the Association constitute a quorum.
c)      Resolutions of the association, except where otherwise specified by the Constitution, are determined by a simple majority of votes of members present. In cases of an equality of votes, the President has the deciding vote.
 
15. AUDITORS

The auditors of the Associations accounts are appointed by the members' the executive committee.

 
16.    BUDGET AND YEARLY BALANCE OF ACCOUNTS
The Executive committee approves the Budget and yearly Balance of Accounts. The yearly Balance of accounts is submitted for approval at an annual General Meeting of the members of the Association.
 
17. GOVERNING BODY RESOLUTIONS
No resolution of the Association or any other governing body can be contrary to a resolution of the General Assembly, or an article of the constitution, or the Law.
 
18. ABSENCES OF MEMBERS OF ASSOCIATION
Any member of the Executive committee who is absent without reasonable and timely excuse, for three consecutive meetings, is disqualified from association membership, and his place filled.
 
19. AMENDMENTS TO THE CONSTITUTION.
The constitution can be amended by the General Assembly, convened expressly for that purpose, following a majority vote of three quarters of the members present at that meeting. However, for amendments to the section concerning the aims of the association, a three quarter majority vote of all the ordinary members of the association is mandatory.
 
20. DISSOLUTION OF THE ASSOCIATION
a) The dissolution of the Association may be decided by the General Assembly, convened expressly for that purpose, following a majority vote of three quarters of the members present at that meeting.
b) In case of dissolution of the association, its assets shall be donated to the ministry of public health on condition the possessions are to be given to medical informatics projects.
 
21. REPRESENTATION OF THE ASSOCIATION
The association is represented in its affairs in and out of court by the President.
 
22. THE ASSOCIATION SEAL
The Association uses an official Seal that has the words "Lebanese Medical Informatics Association" in Arabic and/or English.
 
23. OTHER MATTERS
Any matter not provided for by the articles of the constitution may be regulated by resolutions of the Executive committee, which is the only body empowered to interpret in this way, the provisions of the constitution.
It is implied that any resolution of the Executive committee, as referred to above, may be discussed, approved or rejected at the next Ordinary or Extraordinary General Meeting of members.
